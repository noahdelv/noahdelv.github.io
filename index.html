Deep Divide

A two-player local co-op underwater exploration game built in Unity. One player captains the boat, navigating by radar and fending off pirate raids on the surface, while the other dives below to loot shipwrecks and reefs before their oxygen runs out. Players must hit a daily quota at the merchant, buy upgrades, and push deeper across a five-day run — losing everything they're carrying if they die underwater. I contributed to gameplay systems, UI/UX design, onboarding (tutorial, intro cinematic, contextual hints), diving feel (oxygen HUD, crosshair, underwater effects), and the boat physics rewrite. The game was presented at a university game expo and is in continued development toward a full public release.

Observed

A 2D puzzle-platformer built in Unity centered around a vision-based freeze mechanic. Players use a directional flashlight to freeze enemies and platforms within their line of sight, transforming hazards into traversal tools and puzzle elements. I designed and implemented the core vision-cone detection and freeze system using a PubSub/EventBus architecture to keep gameplay systems modular and decoupled. I also developed player movement and aiming systems, reactive moving platforms, vision-blocking mechanics, a jump system, audio feedback integration, and camera refinements to improve overall clarity and game feel.
